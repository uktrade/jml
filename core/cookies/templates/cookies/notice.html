Cookies

A cookie is a small piece of data that's stored on your computer, tablet, or phone when you visit a website. Most websites need cookies to work properly.

How cookies are used on the {{ SERVICE_NAME }}

We use cookies to:

  • measure how you use the service so it can be improved
  • remember the notifications you've seen so that you're not shown them again
  • temporarily store the answers you give
  • make the service more secure

Cookies used to measure website usage

We use Google Analytics software to collect information about how you use this service. This helps us make sure the service is meeting the needs of its users and helps us make improvements, for example, improving site search.

Google Analytics stores information about:

  • the pages you visit
  • how long you spend on each page
  • how you got to the service
  • what you click on while you're visiting the service

We allow Google to use or share our analytics data. You can find out more about how Google uses this information in its privacy policy.

You can opt out of Google Analytics if you do not want Google to have access to your information.

Google Analytics sets the following cookies:

Google Analytics Cookies
Name Purpose Expires
_ga This helps us count how many people visit Digital Workspace by tracking if you've visited before. 2 years
_gid Identifies you to the service. 24 hours

Storing the answers you've given during your visit (know as a 'session')

We store a session cookie on your computer when you first visit the website, and lets us know which user is accessing a page.

We can then serve you content specifically for you rather than for an anonymous user. Using a session cookie, we can tailor content specifically to you. These are temporary cookies and are usually automatically deleted a short while after you leave Digital Workspace.

We store the page that you last viewed so that when you use the is there anything wrong with this page link to report issues, we know which page you were on.

Session Cookies
Name Purpose Expires
sessionid Allows us to identify your user and serve content aimed specifically at you. 1 hour
last_viewed Allows us to identify the page you last viewed to allow us to know the page your reported an issue with. When your browsing session ends.

Cookies that remember your settings

You may see a banner informing you about cookies. We'll set a cookie so that your computer knows you've already seen it and not to show it again during your browsing session.

Cookies that remember your settings
Name Purpose Expires
cookie_banner_response To know when you have dismissed the cookie banner. When the browsing session ends.

Making the service more secure

We set cookies which prevent attackers from pretending to be you when you do things like submit a form. This makes the service much more secure and protects your personal information.

Security cookies
Name Purpose Expires
csrftoken Ensures that attackers cannot pretend to be other users. 1 year
